## What Is Kanji to Katakana Conversion? 🎌

Japanese text combines **Kanji** (漢字)—Chinese characters representing meaning—with **Katakana** (カタカナ)—phonetic characters representing sound. Converting Kanji to Katakana extracts the pronunciation from Chinese characters and presents it in the angular script used for foreign words, emphasis, and technical terms. For comprehensive information about Katakana, see <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katakana" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia's Katakana article</a>.

### Why Convert Kanji to Katakana? 📝

While Kanji to Hiragana is more common for reading practice, Kanji to Katakana serves specific purposes:

| Use Case | Why Katakana? |
|----------|---------------|
| **Stylistic emphasis** | Katakana adds visual impact |
| **Official forms** | Some documents require Katakana readings |
| **Technical contexts** | Scientific and medical terms |
| **Name badges** | Conferences and professional contexts |
| **Accessibility** | Alternative script for pronunciation |
| **Design purposes** | Logos, signage, creative projects |

### Katakana vs. Hiragana Output 📊

| Script | Appearance | Use for Kanji Conversion |
|--------|------------|-------------------------|
| **Hiragana** | Curved, flowing (ひらがな) | Learning, standard reading |
| **Katakana** | Angular, blocky (カタカナ) | Emphasis, technical, stylistic |

**Same kanji, different output:**
| Kanji | Hiragana | Katakana | Romaji |
|-------|----------|----------|--------|
| 日本語 | にほんご | ニホンゴ | nihongo |
| 学生 | がくせい | ガクセイ | gakusei |
| 先生 | せんせい | センセイ | sensei |

Kanji have multiple readings that affect Katakana conversion:

| Kanji | On'yomi | Katakana | Example Word |
