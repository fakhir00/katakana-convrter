How Katakana Converter Works

A multi-stage pipeline converts English text into phonetically accurate Katakana

1

Normalize

Input is lowercased, cleaned of non-alphabet characters, and tokenized into individual words.

2

Grapheme → Phoneme

Each word is processed through 100+ ordered rules to extract English phonemes (ARPAbet-style).

3

Phoneme → Katakana

Phonemes are mapped to Katakana syllables using a full CV table with epenthetic vowel insertion.

4

Post-Process

Long vowels are normalized, invalid combinations removed, and the output is polished to valid Katakana.

About Katakana Converter

Frequently asked questions about our English to Katakana conversion tool

What is Katakana?

Katakana (カタカナ) is one of the three Japanese writing systems, primarily used for foreign loanwords, onomatopoeia, and technical terms. When English words are borrowed into Japanese, they are written in Katakana to approximate their original pronunciation.

How does this Katakana Converter work?

This converter uses a multi-stage phoneme-based pipeline. It first converts English letters to phonemes using 100+ context-sensitive rules, then maps those phonemes to Katakana syllables following Japanese phonotactic constraints. A loanword dictionary handles irregular words.

Is this Katakana Converter free?

Yes, completely free and open-source. The entire Katakana Converter runs in your browser using pure JavaScript — no server, no API keys, no account required. Your text never leaves your device.
