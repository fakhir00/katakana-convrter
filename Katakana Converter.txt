## What Is a Katakana Converter? 🎌

A **Katakana Converter** is a tool that transforms text from various scripts—English, Kanji, Hiragana, Romaji, and more—into **Katakana** (カタカナ), one of Japan's three writing systems. Katakana is primarily used for foreign words, names, onomatopoeia, and emphasis in Japanese text. For a comprehensive overview, see <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_writing_system" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia's guide to the Japanese writing system</a>.

## Katakana Chart and Reference 📊

### The 46 Basic Katakana Characters

Katakana consists of **46 basic characters** representing Japanese syllables:

**Vowels:**
| ア (a) | イ (i) | ウ (u) | エ (e) | オ (o) |

**K-Row:**
| カ (ka) | キ (ki) | ク (ku) | ケ (ke) | コ (ko) |

**S-Row:**
| サ (sa) | シ (shi) | ス (su) | セ (se) | ソ (so) |

**T-Row:**
| タ (ta) | チ (chi) | ツ (tsu) | テ (te) | ト (to) |

**N-Row:**
| ナ (na) | ニ (ni) | ヌ (nu) | ネ (ne) | ノ (no) |

**H-Row:**
| ハ (ha) | ヒ (hi) | フ (fu) | ヘ (he) | ホ (ho) |

**M-Row:**
| マ (ma) | ミ (mi) | ム (mu) | メ (me) | モ (mo) |

**Y-Row:**
| ヤ (ya) | ユ (yu) | ヨ (yo) |

**R-Row:**
| ラ (ra) | リ (ri) | ル (ru) | レ (re) | ロ (ro) |

**W-Row and N:**
| ワ (wa) | ヲ (wo) | ン (n) |

### Voiced Characters (Dakuten)

Adding dakuten (゛) creates voiced sounds:

| ガ (ga) | ギ (gi) | グ (gu) | ゲ (ge) | ゴ (go) |
| ザ (za) | ジ (ji) | ズ (zu) | ゼ (ze) | ゾ (zo) |
| ダ (da) | ヂ (ji) | ヅ (zu) | デ (de) | ド (do) |
| バ (ba) | ビ (bi) | ブ (bu) | ベ (be) | ボ (bo) |
| パ (pa) | ピ (pi) | プ (pu) | ペ (pe) | ポ (po) |

For a complete reference with stroke orders, <a href="https://www.tofugu.com/japanese/learn-katakana/" rel="nofollow">Tofugu's Katakana guide</a> provides excellent visual resources.
