What Is a Katakana Converter? 🎌

A Katakana Converter (also spelled Katakana Convertor) is a tool that transforms text from various scripts—English, Kanji, Hiragana, Romaji, and more—into Katakana (カタカナ), one of Japan's three writing systems. Katakana is primarily used for foreign words, names, onomatopoeia, and emphasis in Japanese text. For a comprehensive overview, see Wikipedia's guide to the Japanese writing system.

Katakana Chart and Reference 📊

The 46 Basic Katakana Characters

Katakana consists of 46 basic characters representing Japanese syllables:

Vowels: | ア (a) | イ (i) | ウ (u) | エ (e) | オ (o) |

K-Row: | カ | キ (ki) | ク (ku) | ケ (ke) | コ (ko) |

S-Row: | サ (sa) | シ (shi) | ス (su) | セ (se) | ソ (so) |

T-Row: | タ (ta) | チ (chi) | ツ (tsu) | テ (te) | ト (to) |

N-Row: | ナ (na) | ニ (ni) | ヌ (nu) | ネ (ne) | ノ (no) |

H-Row: | ハ (ha) | ヒ (hi) | フ (fu) | ヘ (he) | ホ (ho) |

M-Row: | マ (ma) | ミ (mi) | ム (mu) | メ (me) | モ (mo) |

Y-Row: | ヤ (ya) | ユ (yu) | ヨ (yo) |

R-Row: | ラ (ra) | リ (ri) | ル (ru) | レ (re) | ロ (ro) |

W-Row and N: | ワ (wa) | ヲ (wo) | ン (n) |

Voiced Characters (Dakuten)

Adding dakuten (゛) creates voiced sounds:

| ガ (ga) | ギ (gi) | グ (gu) | ゲ (ge) | ゴ (go) | | ザ (za) | ジ (ji) | ズ (zu) | ゼ (ze) | ゾ (zo) | | ダ (da) | ヂ (ji) | ヅ (zu) | デ (de) | ド (do) | | バ (ba) | ビ (bi) | ブ (bu) | ベ (be) | ボ (bo) | | パ (pa) | ピ (pi) | プ (pu) | ペ (pe) | ポ (po) |

What is Katakana and why do I need to convert to it?

Katakana (カタカナ) is one of three Japanese writing systems, alongside Hiragana and Kanji. It's characterized by angular, straight-lined characters. You need Katakana for:

  • Foreign names — Non-Japanese names are written in Katakana (マイケル = Michael)
  • Loanwords — Foreign-origin words (コーヒー = coffee)
  • Japanese forms — Many documents require Katakana readings
  • Emphasis — Similar to italics in English
  • Technical terms — Scientific and medical vocabulary

What is full-width vs half-width Katakana?

Japanese text uses two character widths:

TypeJapanese TermAppearanceUse
Full-width全角カタカナStandard Japanese text

Full-width Katakana is the standard format for:

  • Official documents
  • Modern Japanese text

Half-width Katakana appears in:
